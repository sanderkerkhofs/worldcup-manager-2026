02 - Data Model, ERD, and ERP Perspective

1. Core Entities

  • User: account with role and optional team assignment.
  • Team: national team metadata and coach info.
  • Player: belongs to team, includes shirt number and status.
  • Round: ordered tournament stage.
  • Match: fixture linking teams, referee, score, status.
  • Goal: scoring event linked to match/player/team.

2. Match Status Vocabulary

  • NOT_STARTED
  • IN_PROGRESS
  • COMPLETED

3. ERD (Mermaid Source)

erDiagram
    TEAM ||--o{ PLAYER : has
    ROUND ||--o{ MATCH : contains
    TEAM ||--o{ MATCH : homeTeam
    TEAM ||--o{ MATCH : awayTeam

    USER o|--|| TEAM : coachUserOptional
    MATCH ||--o{ GOAL : contains
    PLAYER ||--o{ GOAL : scores
    TEAM ||--o{ GOAL : creditedTo

For the full field-level ERD, see 02-data-model-erd.md.

4. Important Constraints

  • User.username unique
  • Team.name unique
  • Round.orderNumber unique
  • Player(teamId, shirtNumber) unique

5. ERP Perspective

  • Master data: teams, players, users
  • Transactional data: matches and goals
  • Process data: round initiation, simulation, completion

6. Why It Fits Knockout Tournaments

  • Round order enables deterministic progression.
  • Status lifecycle supports operational control.
  • Goal events keep scoring auditable and detailed.
  • Team/player split supports coach workflows cleanly.
